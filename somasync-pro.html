Generated SOAP Note
S — Subjective

Client presents with bilateral cervical and upper trapezius pain, rated 6/10, onset approximately 2 weeks prior. Client attributes symptoms to prolonged desk posture and occupational stress. Reports associated tension-type headaches.

O — Objective

Palpation reveals significant hypertonicity in bilateral upper trapezius, left greater than right, with active trigger points in left levator scapulae. Range of motion restricted in left lateral flexion and rotation. No neurological deficits noted.

A — Assessment

M54.2 Cervicalgia with associated myofascial dysfunction (M79.3). Postural strain pattern consistent with prolonged flexion loading. Left-sided levator scapulae involvement contributing to referred cephalic symptoms.

P — Plan

Neuromuscular therapy applied to bilateral upper trapezius. Specific NMT protocol to left levator scapulae and cervical paraspinals. Passive cervical stretching performed. Client education on ergonomic positioning. Reassess in 1 week.
